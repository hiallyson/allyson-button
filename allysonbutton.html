A Button Love Story

by Allyson Escobar

Buttons are used for many things: playing games, accessing doors, opening elevators, pinning and attaching things. For operating computers to saying "Yes, you're on my team" on The Voice. Buttons, I must say, are pretty darn underrated. They may be round, square, trapezoid, whatever you want--but they are almost always reliable, when they want to be. Haven't you ever noticed how much we rely on buttons every day? They give us access to things, they allow us to travel, they hold our clothes together, they control access to the codes to nuclear weapons (or complicated nuclear weapon alert systems). They let us access the screens on our dating apps and help us fall in love. They help us type and code furiously on our keyboards.

So here's to you, dear buttons. Thank you for letting us press you.
